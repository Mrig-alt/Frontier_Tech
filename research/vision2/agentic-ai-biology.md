# Agentic AI Biology Notes (Vision 2)

## Core Thesis
Cursor proved agentic AI can solve tasks autonomously in software.
The next domain is biology.
Agentic AI learns to instruct cells and organoid systems.
Brain mapping gives us human control logic.
Cybersecurity-first because working with human body data requires trust from day one.
Endgame: autonomous body support for long-duration space missions.

## The Predator Line
Cursor proved AI can figure out software autonomously.
Vision 2 asks whether the same loop can figure out biology.
And if it can, whoever builds that platform controls the infrastructure for human survival in space.

## Key Concepts

### Agentic AI
- AI that can plan, execute, and iterate on multi-step tasks autonomously
- Cursor is the proof of concept: AI writes and debugs code without human intervention
- The same agent loop (perceive -> plan -> act -> evaluate) applies to biological systems

### Agentic Biology
- AI agents that design and run biological experiments
- Instruct cells to perform specific functions
- Optimize drug trial protocols autonomously
- Self-improving experimental design

### Brain Mapping as Control Logic
- Human brain maps provide the "operating manual" for biological systems
- Understanding neural circuits = understanding what the body is trying to do
- Agentic AI needs this control logic to know what to optimize toward

## Three Industry Directions

### Space Medicine (NASA/ESA)
- Autonomous body support for long-duration space missions
- Agentic AI manages physiological systems in real-time
- Critical for Mars missions where communication delay = 20 minutes
- Brain mapping + agentic AI = autonomous health monitoring

### Biotech / Synthetic Biology
- Agentic AI drug trial platform
- AI designs and runs experiments, optimizes protocols
- Reduces time and cost of drug discovery
- Brain mapping ensures biological targets are correctly identified

### Embodied Robotics (Boston Dynamics / Hyundai)
- Hybrid body platform
- Agentic AI controls robotic systems using biological control logic
- Brain mapping informs how robots should move and adapt
