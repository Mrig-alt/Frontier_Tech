# Blockchain / Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) Opportunities

## Opportunity B1: Tokenized RWA Treasury and Collateral Platform for Corporates
*   **Technology used:** Permissioned/public blockchain, smart contracts, institutional-grade custody, automated compliance.
*   **Industry/company:** Large corporates and asset managers; banks running tokenization platforms.
*   **The unsolved problem:** RWA tokenization is growing (non-stablecoin market >$15B in 2024), but most products target financial institutions. Corporates lack integrated treasury tools and struggle with fragmented collateral management.
*   **What the product would do:** Offer a tokenized treasury platform where corporates hold tokenized T-bills and RWAs as on-chain assets for just-in-time liquidity. Automates compliance (KYC/AML, tax reporting) with embedded security (institutional custody, smart-contract monitoring).
*   **Why now:** Major asset managers (BlackRock) are commercializing tokenization; regulatory frameworks (MiCA) are clarifying. Corporates feel pain from payment fragmentation.
*   **Key sources/evidence:** RWA tokenization market reports; MAS Project Guardian updates; regulatory developments.

## Opportunity B2: Security‑First Smart‑Contract Platform for Enterprises
*   **Technology used:** Blockchain, formal verification, AI-assisted auditing, secure development lifecycle.
*   **Industry/company:** Enterprises experimenting with Web3, financial institutions entering DeFi/RWA.
*   **The unsolved problem:** Smart-contract vulnerabilities remain the primary cause of Web3 hacks (OWASP Top 10). AI agents can now autonomously discover and exploit vulnerabilities.
*   **What the product would do:** Vertically integrated platform combining secure smart-contract frameworks, automated formal verification, AI-assisted vulnerability scanning, and continuous runtime monitoring. Includes a "blue team-as-a-service" using AI agents for proactive defense.
*   **Why now:** Enterprises want to leverage tokenization but require strong security and compliance assurances against AI-augmented exploitation.
*   **Key sources/evidence:** OWASP Smart Contract Top 10 2025; reports of AI agents exploiting smart contracts.
