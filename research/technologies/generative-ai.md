# Generative AI Opportunities

## Opportunity G1: GenAI for Drug Discovery & Trial Design with Built‑In Compliance
*   **Technology used:** Generative models for molecule design and biology (GenAI), predictive AI for ADMET, secure data-governance layer.
*   **Industry/company:** Pharma and biotech (Big Pharma, AI-native biotechs like Insilico Medicine).
*   **The unsolved problem:** Drug discovery is slow and expensive. While GenAI can accelerate lead identification, integrating models into regulated workflows and protecting sensitive patient data remain major hurdles.
*   **What the product would do:** Provide an end-to-end GxP-ready GenAI platform for drug discovery with foundation models for de novo molecule generation, wrapped in audit-ready experiment tracking. Embed cybersecurity by design: differential privacy for patient data, fine-grained access controls, and monitoring for model-inversion attacks. Connect to LIMS and ELNs.
*   **Why now:** GenAI in drug discovery is a fast-growing market (30%+ CAGR). Regulators and pharma are under pressure to manage costs, making validated, secure AI platforms highly valuable.
*   **Key sources/evidence:** Market reports on GenAI in drug discovery; McKinsey estimates of tens of billions in potential annual value.

## Opportunity G2: Secure GenAI Copilots for Regulated Functions (Risk, Compliance, Legal)
*   **Technology used:** GenAI with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), policy engines, and security monitoring.
*   **Industry/company:** Banks, insurers, telcos, critical infrastructure operators under stringent regulation.
*   **The unsolved problem:** Most organizations lack safe, approved GenAI tooling and worry about data leakage and hallucinations in critical functions like risk, legal, and compliance. Generic GenAI tools are not compliant with EU AI Act high-risk categories.
*   **What the product would do:** Offer domain-specific, "compliance-first" copilots trained on internal policies and regulations. Include integrated data-loss prevention (DLP), red-teaming, and model-behavior monitoring. Provide automated documentation to support AI Act audits and pre-configured templates with built-in access controls.
*   **Why now:** Regulatory deadlines (EU AI Act compliance for high-risk systems by 2026–2027) mandate secure, auditable solutions.
*   **Key sources/evidence:** McKinsey AI reports, EU AI Act documentation, cybersecurity analyses of AI-augmented threats.
