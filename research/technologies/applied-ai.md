# Applied AI (beyond GenAI) Opportunities

## Opportunity A1: AI Platformization for Fragmented Enterprise Pilots
*   **Technology used:** Applied AI, MLOps, policy orchestration; security as core design.
*   **Industry/company:** Large enterprises with many AI pilots (telco, banking, manufacturing), e.g., global banks and telcos highlighted in McKinsey and CIO surveys.
*   **The unsolved problem:** 65–78% of large enterprises report using AI/GenAI, but pilots are fragmented across business units, run on separate RAG stacks and models, and lack unified governance. ~95% of enterprise GenAI pilots fail to deliver measurable business returns because they lack robust governance, data controls, and security.
*   **What the product would do:** Provide a secure AI control plane that standardizes how models are exposed to product teams, including unified authentication, policy enforcement (PII redaction), workload observability, and approved patterns for RAG and agents. Integrate with SIEM/SOAR to monitor for data exfiltration and prompt injection. Offer pre-built "workflow adapters".
*   **Why now (2025–2026):** Enterprise GenAI spend grew to tens of billions, but organizations are hitting governance and security walls. The EU AI Act explicitly requires high-risk AI systems to incorporate robust risk management and cybersecurity controls.
*   **Key sources/evidence:** Menlo Ventures and MIT data on failure rates of GenAI pilots; McKinsey State of AI 2024; EU AI Act high-risk obligations.

## Opportunity A2: AI‑Driven Payment and Treasury Operations Optimization
*   **Technology used:** Predictive and prescriptive AI for cash-flow, routing, and anomaly detection; optional GenAI copilots.
*   **Industry/company:** Large multinationals with fragmented payment systems; global treasuries in manufacturing, ecommerce.
*   **The unsolved problem:** Enterprises juggle dozens of banks and payment rails, leading to fragmented data, manual reconciliation, and delayed liquidity decisions, with hidden costs estimated near 100M USD. Banks report legacy cores as constraints.
*   **What the product would do:** Aggregate multi-bank/multi-rail payment data in near real-time via APIs; build AI models for optimal routing (cost, FX, settlement speed), anomaly detection, and intraday liquidity optimization. Provide a secure "treasury copilot" with bank-grade access controls.
*   **Why now:** Open banking APIs and instant payment rails are expanding. AI for fraud and payment risk is mature, and regulators are pressuring for better operational resilience.
*   **Key sources/evidence:** Oxford/FIS Global estimates of payment friction costs; McKinsey and Deloitte on AI in banking.
