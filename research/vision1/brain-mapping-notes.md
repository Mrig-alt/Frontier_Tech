# Brain Mapping Notes (Vision 1)

## Core Thesis
Google Trends + social media maps population-level emotional signals.
These signals are overlaid onto Google/Harvard brain maps.
IBM Quantum ML classifies which neural circuits activate.
Cybersecurity-first neutral broker platform sells causal cognitive intelligence.

## The Predator Line
Google is already sitting on the most detailed map of human anxiety ever assembled.
The question is who builds the bridge to the clinic before they do.

## Key Research Findings

### Meta TRIBE v2 (2025)
- Trimodal brain encoding model
- Trained on 500 hours of fMRI data from 700 individuals
- Predicts how a human brain responds to almost any video/audio/text input
- Zero-shot generalization to new people and unseen languages
- 23% improvement over previous methods
- Built by Meta, whose core business is selling human attention

### Google Behavioral Data Moat
- Google handles ~80% of global search
- ~136 billion monthly visits (vs 4 billion for ChatGPT)
- Corpus spans intent, emotion, and decision-making across every culture and language
- This is the behavioral signal corpus that quantum ML would need

### IBM + Inclusive Brains BCI Study (June 2025)
- Active, commercially motivated research (not just academic)
- Applying quantum machine learning specifically to BMI classification
- Testing hundreds of thousands of ML algorithm combinations
- Identifying most efficient approaches for interpreting brain activity
- Two companies with deployed quantum hardware and clinical neurotech applications

### Quantum Advantage for Brain Mapping
- QFT reduces MRI reconstruction from hours to minutes (+30% SNR)
- Argonne: quantum-enhanced AI processes teravoxel connectome datasets at 99.7% precision
- Hybrid quantum-classical sentiment analysis outperforms classical ML on large-scale datasets
- Quantum Fourier Transform + cross-linguistic emotional signal detection

## The Convergence Chain
1. Behavioral/sentiment data at global scale (Google, Meta, Microsoft, Baidu)
2. Quantum ML applied to that corpus (pattern recognition beyond classical limits)
3. Cross-referencing with neural data (fMRI, EEG, BCI)
4. Brain function maps emerge from patterns, not just lab scans
5. AI-inferred connectomics at population scale
6. Practical applications: precision psychiatry, BCIs, neuromarketing, cognitive enhancement
