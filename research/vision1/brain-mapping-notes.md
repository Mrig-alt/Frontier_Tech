# Brain Mapping and Behavioral Data

## The Behavioral Data Moat
*   **Google:** Handles roughly 80% of global search (approx. 136 billion monthly visits vs. ChatGPT's ~4 billion). This constitutes a behavioral signal corpus of incomprehensible scale, spanning intent, emotion, and decision-making across all cultures and languages.
*   **Meta:** Sits on massive social behavior, engagement, and emotional response data.

## Meta TRIBE v2 Neural Encoding
*   **Model:** Meta recently released TRIBE v2, a trimodal brain encoding model.
*   **Training Data:** Trained on 500+ hours of fMRI data from 700+ individuals.
*   **Capability:** Predicts how a human brain responds to almost any video, audio, or text input.
*   **Performance:** Achieves zero-shot generalization to new people and unseen languages, representing a 2–3× improvement over previous methods.

## The Convergence Gap
*   While classical ML handles sentiment analysis at a small scale, it fails against high-dimensional, cross-language, global-scale datasets and teravoxel connectomics data.
*   The gap: Combining Google's global sentiment data with Meta's fMRI neural maps using Quantum ML to understand causal cognitive patterns.
