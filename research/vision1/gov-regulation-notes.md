# Government Regulation Notes (Vision 1)

## EU AI Act
- High-risk AI classification for medical/neurological applications
- Explicit cybersecurity requirements for high-risk systems
- Risk management, logging, and documentation obligations
- Compliance deadlines: 2026-2027 for high-risk systems
- Creates regulatory pressure for platformized AI governance in Europe
- Directly relevant: neural data products = high-risk category

## DSA (Digital Services Act)
- Data governance obligations for large platforms
- Relevant to H1 entry point: government compliance tool
- Creates demand for audit-ready, explainable AI systems

## US MIND Act 2025 (Proposed)
- First attempt by Congress to regulate neural data
- CSIS analysis: concerns about workplace attention monitoring, mental privacy
- Corporate commodification of neural signals
- Shows policymakers are alarmed about neural data convergence
- Creates regulatory barrier to entry = competitive advantage

## NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- Influences AI security expectations globally
- Relevant to product design: risk management, documentation, robustness

## NCCoE PQC Migration Roadmap
- Post-quantum cryptography migration requirements
- NIST emphasizes migration must begin immediately, spans a decade+
- Neural data = long-lived sensitive data = must be PQC-protected
- Organizations that delay face systemic legacy risk

## NIST NCCoE Guidance
- Cryptographic inventory and risk scoring
- PQC testing and hybrid scheme evaluation
- Directly relevant to cybersecurity angle of Vision 1

## Implications for Antigravity Product
- Regulatory compliance is a feature, not a burden
- EU AI Act + MIND Act = dual-market opportunity (EU + US)
- PQC-ready encryption for neural data = mandatory from day one
- Audit-ready, explainable AI = competitive differentiator
- Cybersecurity as core value proposition is mandated by regulation
