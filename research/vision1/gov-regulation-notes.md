# Government Regulation and Cybersecurity Drivers

## US MIND Act (2025)
*   **Status:** Proposed in Congress.
*   **Context:** The first major attempt to regulate neural data at the federal level.
*   **Concerns:** Policymakers fear workplace attention monitoring, mental privacy violations, and corporate commodification of neural signals. Neural data is being described as "the black gold of the 21st century."

## EU AI Act (2026-2027)
*   **Classification:** Explicitly classifies biometric and neural inference systems as "high-risk" AI systems.
*   **Requirements:** Demands robust risk management, logging, clear documentation, and strict cybersecurity controls built-in by design.

## Cybersecurity Implication
*   Because neural data is the most sensitive class of personal data imaginable, any platform handling it must be zero-trust and utilize Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) to prevent "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks.
*   A neutral broker platform that natively complies with these regulations using audited, PQC-secured pipelines creates a regulatory moat against big tech monopolies.
